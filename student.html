Student Loans

The student loan system is confusing. With so many different programs, it is difficult to know what to do or what options are available. Due to confusion, many people rely on their loan servicers for help. But loan servicers represent the creditor – not the borrower. If you are having trouble paying your student loans, it is best to learn what help is available to you.

Know What Kind of Loans You Have

You need to know if your loans are federal or private. Federal loans are guaranteed by the government and private loans are not. Once you have determined which loans are federal loans you can determine what federal program that loan falls under. This can make a difference in what hardship or forgiveness programs are available to you.

Learn About Different Loan Forgiveness Programs

Depending on what type of loan you have you may be able to qualify for help in repaying your loans. Some programs include:

  • Assistance for Nurses
  • Assistance for Doctors
  • Assistance for Lawyers
  • Assistance for Teachers
  • Assistance for Other Careers
  • Public Student Loan Forgiveness
  • Forgiveness with Pay As You Earn
  • Federal Perkins Loan Cancellation
  • Military Student Loan Forgiveness
  • Discharge for Special Circumstances
  • Forgiveness with Revised Pay As You Earn
  • Forgiveness with Income-Based Repayment
  • Forgiveness with Income-Contingent Repayment

Know Your Options

The key in dealing with your student loans is to know your options. As with all areas of personal finance, there are professionals who can help you if you don't have the time to learn it all on your own. Most importantly, learn about your options before you need help so that you do not go into default.
