Most Americans Can't Afford a $500 Expense

Consumers need access to credit in order to pay for unexpected expenses. About half of all households have an unexpected expense of $500 or more each year. Many consumers use credit to pay for day to day expenses as well. When this is the case, some do not have enough available credit to cover unexpected expenses even with credit.

This problem is made worse by how companies use credit reports to determine everything from interest rates to whether or not you can get insurance. Employers can use credit reports in making employment desicions, including whether or not an employee will be retained or promoted.

Unfortunately, many credit reports have major inaccuracies significant enough to move a consumer into a lower credit tier, increasing cost to the consumer in many ways. Seemingly minor inaccuracies, such as an incorrect telephone number or addresse, make the consumer more likely to be an identity theft victim.
